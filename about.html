Why the platform exists

Wildlife issues can feel urgent. Choosing a provider should still feel organized.

Many homeowners notice signs suddenly: scratching above a ceiling, movement near a roofline, nesting material in a vent, or animal activity around a garage. The next step can feel confusing because different situations may require different provider experience, methods, timing, and local rules.

WILD GUARD helps simplify that first step. Instead of presenting ourselves as a contractor, we operate as a matching platform that helps homeowners compare local independent provider categories and request relevant options.

Aggregator model

What WILD GUARD does — and what it does not do.

The platform is designed to make provider comparison more practical while keeping homeowner responsibility clear.

We help organize the first step

Homeowners can share their wildlife concern, ZIP code, and basic details so the request can be directed toward relevant independent provider categories.

We connect, not contract

WILD GUARD may help connect homeowners with local provider options. Any scheduling, pricing, inspection, service agreement, or hiring decision happens directly between the homeowner and provider.

We encourage direct verification

Homeowners should confirm license requirements, insurance, service scope, methods, fees, availability, and written terms directly before choosing a provider.

We are not a wildlife contractor

WILD GUARD does not inspect homes, remove animals, seal entry points, clean attics, repair damage, or guarantee provider work.

About the platform

Helpful questions about WILD GUARD.

These details explain how the platform works and what homeowners should confirm independently.

Is WILD GUARD a direct wildlife removal company?

No. WILD GUARD is a free matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local wildlife service providers, but we do not perform removal, exclusion, cleanup, or repair work ourselves.

Why was WILD GUARD created?

WILD GUARD was created to give homeowners a clearer first step when they notice wildlife activity around a home and want to compare relevant provider categories.

Does WILD GUARD choose the provider for me?

No. Homeowners remain responsible for reviewing provider details, asking questions, and deciding whether to contact or hire any independent provider.

What should I verify before hiring a provider?

Verify license and insurance requirements, service area, methods, pricing, inspection fees, written scope, follow-up terms, exclusions, and any local wildlife rules that may apply.

Does WILD GUARD guarantee provider work?

No. Providers are independent, and WILD GUARD does not warrant or guarantee any work performed by providers listed, contacted, or matched through the site.
