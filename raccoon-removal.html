Service overview

Raccoon activity often starts around roof access points.

Homeowners may notice raccoons near chimneys, roof edges, soffits, attic vents, garages, decks, or crawlspace openings. In some cases, noises may be heard at night or early morning, especially around attic spaces or exterior access points.

WILD GUARD helps organize the first step by connecting your request with independent local provider options that may be familiar with raccoon activity, inspection questions, humane methods, and exclusion planning.

Before choosing

Questions to ask raccoon removal providers.

Raccoon-related projects can vary by access point, location, timing, and prevention needs. Ask clear questions before scheduling or hiring any independent provider.

01

How will the access point be evaluated?

Ask whether the provider reviews chimneys, soffits, fascia, vents, roof intersections, attic spaces, crawlspaces, or garages.

02

What humane approach is proposed?

Discuss removal approach, timing, whether young animals may be present, local wildlife rules, and how the provider avoids repeat access.

03

What is included in the estimate?

Review inspection fees, removal scope, exclusion materials, cleanup needs, follow-up visits, exclusions, warranties, and payment terms.

04

What should the homeowner verify?

Confirm required licensing, insurance, service area, emergency availability, written terms, and whether repair work is included or separate.

What may affect provider choice

Raccoon concerns may involve removal, exclusion, and cleanup questions.

Provider fit can depend on where raccoon activity appears, how easy the area is to access, whether structural openings exist, whether attic cleanup is discussed, and whether exclusion work is part of the quote.

Entry point location

Chimneys, soffits, attic vents, roof intersections, crawlspaces, and garages can affect provider recommendations.

Timing and activity pattern

Night movement, repeated visits, seasonal activity, or possible nesting can change what questions homeowners should ask.

Exclusion and prevention

Ask whether sealing, screening, cleanup, follow-up, or repair recommendations are included or quoted separately.

Raccoon removal questions

Before comparing raccoon providers.

These questions help homeowners compare independent provider options without assuming WILD GUARD performs the work directly.

Does WILD GUARD remove raccoons directly?

No. WILD GUARD is a matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local providers but do not inspect homes, remove raccoons, seal entry points, or guarantee provider work.

What should I ask a raccoon removal provider?

Ask about inspection steps, humane methods, possible entry points, young animals, licensing where required, insurance, pricing, exclusion, cleanup, and written quote details.

What affects raccoon removal pricing?

Pricing may depend on the access point, attic or chimney access, number of openings, removal complexity, exclusion materials, cleanup needs, follow-up visits, and provider availability.

Should I ask about exclusion after raccoon activity?

Yes. If raccoons accessed the structure, homeowners should ask providers whether sealing, screening, repairs, or prevention steps are recommended and whether those items are included in the quote.
