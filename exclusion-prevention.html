Service overview

Exclusion work focuses on reducing repeat wildlife access.

After wildlife activity appears, homeowners often need to understand how animals accessed the structure and whether openings should be sealed, screened, repaired, or monitored. Exclusion and prevention work may involve rooflines, vents, soffits, fascia, crawlspaces, decks, chimneys, gaps, and exterior weak points.

WILD GUARD helps route your request toward independent local provider options that may be familiar with entry point evaluation, prevention planning, exclusion materials, and follow-up discussions.

Before choosing

Questions to ask exclusion and prevention providers.

Exclusion work can vary widely by material, access, animal type, and repair needs. Compare written details carefully before hiring any independent provider.

01

How will entry points be identified?

Ask whether the provider evaluates vents, soffits, fascia, roof intersections, chimneys, crawlspaces, garage gaps, decks, and other exterior openings.

02

What sealing materials are proposed?

Discuss screening, flashing, mesh, sealants, repair materials, one-way exclusion steps, and whether materials are appropriate for the animal type.

03

What is included in the estimate?

Review inspection fees, labor, materials, number of openings, cleanup recommendations, follow-up visits, warranty terms, exclusions, and payment timing.

04

What should be verified directly?

Confirm required licensing, insurance, repair scope, service area, product details, warranty language, written terms, and whether additional contractors may be needed.

Exclusion questions

Before comparing exclusion providers.

These questions help homeowners compare independent provider options while keeping WILD GUARD’s role clear as a matching platform.

Does WILD GUARD perform exclusion work directly?

No. WILD GUARD is a matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local providers but do not inspect homes, seal entry points, install screening, repair damage, or guarantee provider work.

What should I ask an exclusion provider?

Ask about entry point inspection, sealing materials, repair scope, animal type, timing, licensing where required, insurance, pricing, warranty details, and follow-up.

Are exclusion estimates usually free?

Some providers may offer free estimates, while others may charge for inspections, access evaluations, or travel. Confirm any fees directly before scheduling.

What affects exclusion pricing?

Pricing may depend on the number of openings, access difficulty, materials used, repair needs, cleanup recommendations, follow-up visits, warranty terms, and provider availability.

Should exclusion happen before or after removal?

It depends on the situation and animal type. Homeowners should ask providers whether removal, one-way exclusion, timing, or sealing steps should happen first, and how the provider avoids trapping animals inside.
