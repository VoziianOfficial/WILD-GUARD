Service overview

Squirrel activity often involves small exterior openings.

Homeowners may notice scratching, running sounds, chewed fascia, activity near vents, movement along gutters, or signs around attic entry points. Squirrels can use surprisingly small openings, so provider comparison often includes both removal and exclusion questions.

WILD GUARD helps route your request toward independent local provider options that may be familiar with squirrel activity, attic access, vent openings, roofline gaps, and prevention-focused next steps.

Before choosing

Questions to ask squirrel removal providers.

Squirrel-related projects can involve attic access, small gaps, chewed materials, nesting, and exclusion work. Compare provider details carefully before scheduling.

01

How will the entry points be checked?

Ask whether the provider reviews roof edges, soffits, vents, fascia boards, gutters, attic access, and small openings around the structure.

02

What removal approach is proposed?

Discuss humane methods, timing, whether nesting may be involved, and how the provider plans to reduce repeat entry.

03

What does the estimate include?

Review inspection fees, removal scope, exclusion materials, vent screening, follow-up visits, cleanup, exclusions, and payment terms.

04

What credentials should be verified?

Confirm required licensing, insurance, service area, written terms, availability, and whether repair or sealing work is included.

What may affect provider choice

Squirrel concerns often depend on access, nesting, and prevention.

Provider fit can depend on where activity is happening, how many openings are visible, whether attic access is needed, whether nesting material is present, and whether exclusion work is part of the proposed service.

Exterior opening size

Small gaps near vents, soffits, fascia, and roof intersections may require specific inspection and sealing questions.

Sound pattern

Fast daytime movement, scratching, or repeated attic sounds may help providers understand possible squirrel activity.

Exclusion scope

Ask whether vent screening, sealing, follow-up, cleanup, or repair recommendations are included or quoted separately.

Squirrel removal questions

Before comparing squirrel providers.

These questions help homeowners compare independent provider options while keeping WILD GUARD’s role clear as a matching platform.

Does WILD GUARD remove squirrels directly?

No. WILD GUARD is a matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local providers but do not inspect homes, remove squirrels, seal openings, or guarantee provider work.

What should I ask a squirrel removal provider?

Ask about inspection steps, humane methods, attic access, vent or fascia openings, licensing where required, insurance, pricing, exclusion, cleanup, and written quote details.

Are squirrel removal estimates usually free?

Some providers may offer free estimates, while others may charge for inspections, travel, attic evaluations, or roofline checks. Confirm any fees directly before scheduling.

What affects squirrel removal pricing?

Pricing may depend on attic access, number of openings, exclusion materials, cleanup needs, follow-up visits, roofline complexity, and provider availability.

Should I ask about sealing after squirrel activity?

Yes. If squirrels accessed vents, fascia, soffits, or attic areas, ask whether sealing, screening, repairs, or prevention steps are recommended and whether they are included in the quote.
