Service overview

Wildlife removal starts with identifying the concern clearly.

General wildlife activity may involve noises in walls, animals near rooflines, nesting material, droppings, crawlspace activity, or repeated movement around a property. Different providers may approach inspection, removal, exclusion, and prevention differently.

WILD GUARD helps homeowners organize that first step by connecting requests with independent local providers who may be suited to the type of activity described.

Before choosing

Questions to ask wildlife removal providers.

A careful comparison helps homeowners understand what each independent provider includes and what may require separate approval or additional cost.

01

How will the provider inspect the issue?

Ask whether they evaluate attics, crawlspaces, rooflines, vents, chimneys, garages, or other areas related to the reported activity.

02

What humane methods are discussed?

Ask about removal approach, animal type, timing, local wildlife rules, and whether exclusion or follow-up is recommended.

03

What is included in the estimate?

Review inspection fees, removal scope, materials, cleanup, entry point sealing, follow-up visits, exclusions, and payment terms.

04

What credentials should be verified?

Homeowners should confirm required licensing, insurance, service area, availability, and written terms directly with the provider.

Activity type affects provider fit
What may affect provider choice

Not every wildlife concern requires the same provider approach.

Provider fit can depend on the animal type, where activity is happening, whether young animals may be present, how many access points exist, and whether the project includes exclusion or cleanup.

Animal type

Raccoons, squirrels, bats, birds, opossums, and other wildlife may require different provider experience.

Location of activity

Attics, crawlspaces, chimneys, decks, vents, and rooflines can affect inspection and access needs.

Prevention scope

Some projects may involve entry point sealing, screening, follow-up, or additional repair recommendations.

Wildlife removal questions

Before comparing wildlife providers.

Use these questions to understand the platform model and compare independent provider options more carefully.

Does WILD GUARD remove wildlife directly?

No. WILD GUARD is a matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local providers but do not inspect homes, remove animals, seal entry points, or guarantee work.

What should I ask a wildlife removal provider?

Ask about inspection steps, humane methods, animal type, licensing where required, insurance, pricing, exclusion work, cleanup, follow-up, and written quote details.

Are wildlife removal estimates usually free?

Some providers may offer free estimates, while others may charge for inspections or travel. Confirm any fees directly with each independent provider before scheduling.

How do I know if a provider serves my area?

WILD GUARD can help route your request based on location, but homeowners should confirm service area and availability directly with each provider.
