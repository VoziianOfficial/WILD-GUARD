Before choosing

Questions to ask bird control providers.

Bird-related projects may involve access, prevention products, nesting rules, cleaning questions, and exterior surface protection. Compare provider details before scheduling.

01

How will the bird activity be evaluated?

Ask whether the provider reviews gutters, vents, ledges, rooflines, chimneys, solar panel edges, exterior beams, and visible nesting or roosting areas.

02

What humane control options are discussed?

Discuss prevention methods, deterrent options, exclusion materials, timing, and whether local nesting or species rules may apply.

03

What is included in the estimate?

Review inspection fees, access equipment, prevention materials, cleanup recommendations, follow-up visits, exclusions, and payment terms.

04

What should be verified directly?

Confirm required licensing, insurance, service area, local compliance, written terms, product details, and warranty or maintenance policies.

Bird control questions

Before comparing bird control providers.

These questions help homeowners compare independent provider options while keeping WILD GUARD’s role clear as a matching platform.

Does WILD GUARD provide bird control directly?

No. WILD GUARD is a matching platform. We help homeowners connect with independent local providers but do not inspect homes, install deterrents, remove nests, clean surfaces, or guarantee provider work.

What should I ask a bird control provider?

Ask about inspection steps, humane methods, nesting rules, prevention products, access needs, licensing where required, insurance, pricing, cleanup, and written quote details.

Are bird control estimates usually free?

Some providers may offer free estimates, while others may charge for inspections, access evaluations, or travel. Confirm any fees directly before scheduling.

What affects bird control pricing?

Pricing may depend on the activity location, access difficulty, product type, cleanup needs, prevention scope, follow-up visits, and provider availability.

Should I ask about nesting rules?

Yes. Some bird situations may involve active nests or protected species. Homeowners should ask each provider what local rules or timing considerations apply before scheduling.
